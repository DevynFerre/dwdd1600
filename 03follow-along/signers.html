Who Were the Signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress and the first to sign the Declaration of Independence. His large, bold signature became iconic and symbolized defiance against British rule.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Jefferson articulated the colonies' reasons for seeking independence and emphasized natural rights such as life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.
Benjamin Franklin
A respected statesman, inventor, and diplomat. Franklin helped edit the Declaration and later played a crucial role in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Adams was instrumental in persuading others to support the Declaration and later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A key figure in organizing colonial resistance to British policies. Samuel Adams was a leader of the Sons of Liberty and helped rally support for independence.

Each of these people played a crucial role in the founding of the United States of America.
